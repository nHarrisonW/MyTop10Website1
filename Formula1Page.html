Top Formula One Tracks (as of 2022)

Formula One is often regarded amongst racing fans as the highest class of racing. Today, cars push over 1,000 horespower and weave through three mile tracks in under two minutes, all with a V6 turbo charged hybrid engine. From strict diets for drivers to countless hours testing everything from aerodynamics to weight balance, this sport stands for much more than that. 72 years of racing has built legendary names such as Enzo Ferarri, Bruce McLaren, and Michael Shumacher, just to name a few. Just as important as the teams and drivers, this sport has also brought race fans from around the world to drive the tracks that these cars race on. Today we'll be going through some of my favorite tracks as of 2022. This list does not acknowledge different eras of racing outside of the hybrid era (cars that are smaller, or do not feature DRS). I'm Harrison, and I have been into racing since a young child. In 2006, I loved Fernando Alonso's Championship winning Renault R25, but since then, my favorite racing team has been Williams and Mercedes-AMG Petronas, and although neither teams are doing well in the Drivers' Championship and the Constructors' Championship, I am still faithful that we will be back on top soon than later! I have many hours of kart-racing experience and thousands of hours sim-racing on the worlds best tracks. Although I have never driven them in real life, these tracks are ranked by the amount of fun they are to DRIVE as well as watch!

Honorable Mention: Monaco Grand Prix

Known as one of racings oldest circuits, Circuit de Monaco is a race older than Formula One itself. The first race was held here in 1929 under Prince Louis II. Back then, the cars didn't even break 150 horsepower and the race was won by William Grover-Williams in his Bugatti. Almost 100 years later, the circuit has become a staple of motorsports and is commonly refered to as racings most difficult circuit. Twelve seat-gripping turns, this race is controversial for many reasons. First, being a city circuit, there is limited space for barriers that can absorb the impact of a car and protect the drivers, making it fairly unsafe by todays standards as the cars become larger and larger. Larger cars also make for a more boring race day in Monaco because much of the track is simply too narrow to fit two cars at race speed. In the last 10 years, Monaco has seen 101 racers overtake another driver, and most of these are due to pit penaltys or failures and usually do not take place on the track. For comparison , the Belgian Grand Prix in 2022 had 74 overtakes in total. In 2016, the Chinese Grand Prix in Shanghai saw a staggering 161 overtakes, making a new record. What makes this race exciting to fans today is the qualifying round the day before. Teams send their drivers to push every bit of performance out of their car in order to get the best lap time, and thus the best starting position. It is absolutely crucial on a track were you are unlikely to overtake. Here in 2005, we see Ferarri legend and fan-favorite Kimi Raikkonen set his final qualifying lap of the day to take pole postition from Fernando Alonso's Renault R25 by almost half a second, and coming in over an entire second in front of his teammate, Juan Montoya. This is just one example of why Raikkonen's name lives on past his departure of the sport. This is commonly regarded as the most epic Formula One qualifying session of all time, and lives in infamy to this day.

1. Jeddah Corniche Circuit

(Saudi Arabian Grand Prix)

Mercedes dominates in 2021

Lewis Hamilton takes first place at Jeddah's inaugural race, keeping his chase for his record-breaking eighth world title alive in 2021. Tight, fast turns, long straights and three DRS zones make for thrilling qualifying sessions as well as some pretty intense battles on the track!

Night racing

Because of Saudi Arabia's intense heat, races are held at night to minimalise the amount of heat amage done to the tires.

Dangers of the track

Multiple drivers have complained about the intensity of the turns in combination with the minimal runoff areas and lack of gravel traps to slow drivers if and when their cars lose traction. Here is Nicolas Latifi exiting his Williams FW44 after crashing at Jeddah this year.

2. The Autodromo Internazionale Enzo e Dino Ferrari (Imola)

(Emilia-Romagna Grand Prix)

Ayrton Senna leads the pack

Two names that will forever be attached, here is three time world champion Ayrton Senna, who tragically had a fatal accident on this track in 1994, leading the race, then known as the San Marino Grand Prix in 1988. This track is legendary for much more than that, however, being named after Enzo Ferrai and his late son 'Dino' this is the only track to ever replace Monza as the Itaian Grand Prix.

Hair-razing turns

Chamionship leaders Hamilton and Verstappen battling for position in 2021, this track has been known for it's many legendary battles through Tamburello, one of the fastest turns in all of F1.

3. Silverstone Circuit

(British Grand Prix)

British fans roaring as Hamilton takes the lead on Lap 1

Britain has become home to many race teams such as Williams, McLaren, and Lotus just to name a few, as well as some of Formula One's best drivers as well. The first 'British Grand Prix' was held here in 1926, long before Formula One's inaugural season in 1950. Since then Silverstone has been on the F1 calender every single year as the host of the British Grand Prix.

Lewis Hamilton celebrates victory at his home Grand Prix

A special event for any driver, it is always a goal to come away with a victory at your home Grand Prix, and that's exactly what seven-time Formula One world champion Lewis Hamilton did in 2021 for the eighth time in his career.

4. Red Bull Ring

(Austrian Grand Prix)

Red Bull Statue

In 2004, billionaire and owner of 'Red Bull' Dietrich Mateschitz bought what was known as the A1-Ring, and in the same year, created 'Red Bull Racing', entering the Constructor's Chamionship and renaming the circuit to the 'Red Bull Ring.' Since then, Red Bull has gone on to make a name for themselves, winning the Constructors' Championship in 2010, 2011,2012, 2013, and most recentely 2022 with 2 races still remaining in the season.

Fans blazed in orange cheering on Dutchman Max Verstappen

Being so close to The Netherlands, the Dutch fanbase of Speilberg, Austria is always enflamed in the Royal Dutch color of orange in support of one the greatest driving talents we have seen in recent years, two-time Formula One world champion Max Verstappen.

Speilberg, Austria

Possibly home to the most beautiful scenery in all of Formula One's calender, Speilberg is also the HQ of the Red Bull Racing team.

5. Circuit de Spa-Francorchamps

(Belgian Grand Prix)

Belgian Grand Prix (1960)

Amongst the most famous tracks on this list, Spa-Francorchamps is undoubtedly one of racings most adored circuits. Only missing a few seasons of F1 since it held it's first F1 race in the inaugural 1950 season, the Belgian Grand Prix has become a staple event in Formula One.

Eau Rouge

This is one of racings most demanding sections, 'Eau Rouge' sees today's cars scream through at almost 190+ miles per hour. This turn is known as a 'flat out' turn because drivers do not lift from the throttle mostly at all entering and exiting the turn. For that reason, the drivers must have complete faith in their team and car. Even the slightest slip in the tires could be catastrophic to a driver. Most recently, Anthoine Hubert lost control of his car while going though Eau Rouge at over 160 miles per hour due to a missing front wing. By the time Hubert hit the wall, his car was still travelling at over 130 miles per hour with a g-force equivalent to 33.7g. His car was then hit by another racer traveling about 133 miles per hour, causing a maximum g-force of over 80g. Hubert did not survive the second impact, and just goes to show the dangers of Eau Rouge. Since then, new safety measures continue to be brought forward as racing continues on this legenary track.

6. Autodromo Nazionale di Monza

(Italian Grand Prix)

The heart of Italian racing

Every single Formula One Italian Grand Prix has been held here at Monza, with the single exception of a replacement by Imola in 1980, and has also been featured in every single F1 season besides 1980. Racing has deep roots here in Italy, being home to Constructors like Ferarri and Alfa Romeo, many don't know that Ferarri is actually a race team, then car manufacturer for the public. In fact, the only reason Ferarri started selling cars to the public was to fund their race team.

Ferarri's Monza livery

In 2022, Ferrari fitted their drivers with yellow race suits for the weekend, as well as this custom livery featuring a yellow stripe down the back to celebrate the storied car companies 75th year in business.

7. Suzuka International Racing Course

(Japanese Grand Prix)

Senna and Prost collide in thier battle for the title (1989)

In one of the most famous title fights in all of racing teammates Ayrton Senna and Alain Prost both needed to win the final race of the season at Suzuka Circuit. After chasing for much of the race, Senna believed he had found his chance to pass Prost for the lead. In lap 46 of 53, Senna dove into the final chicane of the lap in an attempt to overtake his teammate. Senna was not able to make the braking zone, causing Prost to turn into him, stopping both cars dead in their tracks. Senna was able to keep his car running and was pushed back into the race by stewards, where he was able to regain the lead, only to be disqualified for his collision with Prost. This was a catalyst event among a season of conflicts between the two teammates that drove Prost to leave McLaren.

Senna and Prost colide again (1990)

After joining Ferarri next year, Prost took pole position to start the race. Being one of the most difficult tracks to overtake on, Senna decided to dive in at turn 1 in an attempt to put Prost behind him before they collided, causing them both not to finish, thus giving Senna his second world title. Many fans thought this was a dirty move by Senna and accuse the racing legend of intentionally crashing into his ex-teammate in order to win the title. Either way, fans were robbed of a surely epic final race of the 1990 season.

8. Yas Marina Circuit

(Abu Dhabi Grand Prix)

Red Bull Racing (2021)

Staying on the theme of epic final races, Yas Marina Circuit, the home of the Abu Dhabi Grand Prix hosted the last race of the 2021 season, where both Red Bull's Max Verstappen and Mercedes' Lewis Hamilton both needed to win in order to take the Drivers' Championship.

Verstappen passes Hamilton in the final lap

After a "lucky" safety car caused a reset in the racing, Verstappen was able to catch up to championship rival Lewis Hamilton. Although the championship's governing body, the FIA, said that 'human error' by race directors caused a breach in safety car protocol, Verstappen was not aware and was awarded the championship for the 2021 season, the most dramatic season finale of the hybrid era.

Under the Lights!

Abu Dhabi has become another home to night racing due to the extreme heat of the surrouning regions. This unique race track encloses the Yas Hotel, a 5 star luxury hotel.

9. Nurburgring

(German or European Grand Prix)

Fan Favorite!

Home grand prix to perhaps motorsports most beloved driver, Michael Shumacher is seen here sitting in a Mercedes W196, the companies car entered in the Formula One 1954 and 1955 season.

Difficulty: Insane!

Widely known as one of racings most difficult tracks, the grand prix part of the track holds 15 intense turns as well as frequent elevation changes. Another track with historical value, the Nurburgring is an extension of the famous 'Nordshleife,' which was also nick-named 'The Green Hell' by legend Jackie Stewart for it's 154 turns that weave through the lush German mountains. This upper part of the track known as the 'Nordshleife' literally translates to 'northen loop' and people travel from all over the world to take their cars here. One day I will too!

10. The Baku City Circuit

(Azerbaijan or European Grand Prix)

Castles!

This castle chicane has quickly become one of the most notorious turns in all of the Formula One calender. Drivers weave through here one by one at mind-boggling speeds at one of the fastest circuits.

Baku City

Baku is the capital city of the beautiful nation of Azerbaijan. Baku gives a new definition to city circuits. Reaching speeds north of 360 kilometers per hour and a mix of 20 turns, this track also has reminiscence of Monaco, while still giving drivers enough space to overtake each other, especially in today's Formula One cars, which are much bigger and faster than previous seasons.
