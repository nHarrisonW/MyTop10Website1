Top Formula One Tracks (as of 2022)

Formula One is often regarded amongst racing fans as the highest class of racing. Today, cars push over 1,000 horespower and weave through tracks three mile tracks in under two minutes, all with a V6 turbo charged hybrid engine. From strict diets for drivers to countless hours testing everything from aerodynamics to weight balance, this sport stands for much more than that. 72 years of racing has built legendary names such as Enzo Ferarri, Bruce McLaren, and Michael Shumacher, just to name a few. Just as important as the teams and drivers, this sport has also brought race fans from around the world to drive the tracks that these cars race on. Today we'll be going through some of my favorite tracks as of 2022. This list does not take acknowledge different eras of racing outside of the hybrid era (Cars that are smaller, or do not feature DRS). I'm Harrison, and I have been into racing since a young child. In 2006, I loved Fernando Alonso's Championship winning Renault R25, but since then, my favorite racing team is Williams and Mercedes-AMG Petronas, and although neither teams are doing well in the Drivers' Championship and the Constructors' Championship, I am still faithful that we will be back on top soon than later!

Mercedes dominates in 2021

Lewis Hamilton takes takes first place at Jeddah's inaugural race, keeping his chase for his record-breaking eigth world title alive in 2021.

Night racing

Because of Saudi Arabia's intense heat, races are held at night to minimalise the amount of heat amage done to the tires.

Dangers of the track

Multiple drivers have complained about the intensity of the turns in combination with the minimal runoff areas and lack of gravel traps to slow drivers if and when their cars lose traction.

1. Jeddah Corniche Circuit

(Saudi Arabian Grand Prix)

Ayrton Senna leads the pack

Two names that will forever be attached, here is three time worldAyrton Senna, who tragically had a fatal accident on this track in 1994, leading the race, then known as the San Marino Grand Prix in 1988. This track is legendary for much more than that, however, being named after Enzo Ferrai and his late son 'Dino' this is the only track to ever replace Monza as the Itaian Grand Prix.

Hair-razing turns

Chamionship leaders Hamilton and Verstappen battling for position in 2021, this track has been know for it's many legendary battles through Tamburello, one of the fastest turns in all of F1

2. The Autodromo Internazionale Enzo e Dino Ferrari (Imola)

(Emilia-Romagna Grand Prix)

British fans roaring as Hamilton takes the lead on Lap 1

Britain has become home to many race teams such as Williams and Lotus to name a few, as well as some of Formula One's best drivers as well.

Lewis Hamilton celebrates victory at his home Grand Prix

A special event for any driver, it is always a goal to come away with a victory at your home Grand Prix, and that's exactly what seven-time Formula One world champion Lewis Hamilton did 2021 for the eigth time in his career.

3. Silverstone Circuit

(British Grand Prix)

Red Bull Statue

In 2004, billionaire and owner of 'Red Bull' Dietrich Mateschitz bought what was known as the A1-Ring, and in the same year, created 'Red Bull Racing', entering the Constructor's Chamionship and renaming the circuit to the 'Red Bull Ring' Since then, Red Bull has gone on to make a name for themselves, winning the Constructors' Championship in 2010, 2011,2012, 2013, and most recentely 2022 with 2 races still remaining in the season.

Fans blazed in orange cheering on Dutchman Max Verstappen

Being so close to The Netherlands, the Dutch fanbase of Speilberg, Austria is always enflamed in the Royal Dutch color orange in support of one the greatest driving talents we have seen in recent years, two-time Formula One world champion Max Verstappen.

Speilberg, Austria

Possibly home to the most beautiful scenery in all of Formula One's calender, Speilberg is also the HQ of the Red Bull Racing team.

4. Red Bull Ring

(Austrian Grand Prix)

Belgian Grand Prix (1960)

Amongst the most famous tracks on this list, Spa-Francorchamps is undoubtedly one of racings most adored circuits. Only missing a few seasons of F1 since it held it's first F1 race in the inaugural 1950 season, the Belgian Grand Prix has become a staple event in Formula One.

Eau Rouge

This is one of racings most demanding sections, 'Eau Rouge' sees todays cars scream through at almost 190+ miles per hour. This turn is known as a 'flat out' turn because drivers do not lift from the throttle mostly at all entering and exiting the turn.
